Beautiful Japan

Have you ever been to beautiful cities like Kyoto and Shibuya? Or tried Kimono and Yukata? Japan owns those all, check here out!"

Fushimi Inari Taisha is the main temple of Inari, the god of rice, sake, prosperity, protector of business, traders and factories. It is located under Mount Inari.

Mount Fuji is 3,776 meters high above the sea surface and visited by 200 to 300 thousand climbers every year.

Sannomachi Street, there are charming dark brown houses from the Edo era. Most of them still function as places to live, and some others have turned into restaurants, cafes, or gift shops.

Kyoto is home to several Japanese cultural rulers including some of the best kimono makers, also famous for food, music, and crafts.

Hachiko Statue is the Shibuya mascot located in front of Shibuya station. This statue depicts Japanese culture, which is on time, never give up patience and loyalty.

Shirakawa-go is located in the Sho-gawa River Valley, in the Ryohaku mountain valley, bordering Ishikawa prefecture.

Murodo is located 2450 meters above sea level, Murodo is the highest point along the Tateyama Kurobe Mountain route, which offers fantastic views of the Tateyama Mountain.

Nakamise Shopping Street is located along the road in Asakusa, this Japanese-style shopping street is shopping paradise for traditional snacks and souvenirs.

Mishima Skywalk is located in the city of mishima, lies along 400 meters with beautiful scenery around it including being able to see Mount Fuji from afar.

Yukata is a traditional Japanese dress which is a casual outfit from Kimono for the summer and is made of cotton.

Kurobe Dam is located at an altitude of about 2,000 meters, and the deepest dam in Japan, with the beauty of the Tateyama Kurobe Alpine

Kiyomizu Dera Temple, which means the temple of clean water, is a magnificent temple located in the mountains of Ottawa
